PCBA processing process

  1. Printing paste

    The squeegee pushes the solder paste along the surface of the stencil. When the solder paste reaches an open area of ​​the stencil, the downward pressure exerted by the squeegee forces the solder paste to fall through the stencil opening area onto the PCB.

  2. Applying a binder

    The double-sided assembled PCB board is used to prevent the bottom surface mounting component during wave soldering or the large integrated circuit component at the bottom of the double-sided reflow soldering from being melted and dropped, and the adhesive is used to stick the component. In addition, in order to prevent the position of heavy components from moving when the PCB is transferred, it is also necessary to stick it with an adhesive.

  3. Component placement

    This process uses an automated placement machine to pick up surface mount components from the feeder and accurately mount them on a printed PCB.

  4. Pre-weld and post-weld inspection

    Before the component is reflowed, it is necessary to carefully check whether the component is well mounted and the position is not offset. After the soldering is completed, solder joints and other quality defects need to be verified before the assembly enters the next process step.

  5. Reflow soldering will

    After the component is placed on the solder, the solder on the pad is melted by a flow splicing process using a thermal convection technique to form a mechanical and electrical interconnection between the component leads and the pads.

  6. Component insertion

    For through-hole components and surface mount components that cannot be mounted on certain machines, such as some cartridge electrolytic capacitors, connectors, push button switches, and metal terminal electrode components (MELF), for manual insertion or use Automatic instrumentation for component insertion.

  7. Wave soldering

    Wave soldering is mainly used to solder through-hole components. When the PCB board passes over the wave crest, the solder wets the lead wires leaking from the bottom surface of the PCB board, and the solder is attracted into the plating socket to form a tight interconnection of the components and the pads.

  8. Cleaning

    Optional process. When the solder paste contains organic components such as rosin and lipids, the residue formed by combining them with the water in the atmosphere after soldering has strong chemical corrosion, and the reliability of the circuit connection is hindered when left on the PCB. Therefore, these chemicals must be thoroughly cleaned.

  9. Maintenance

    This is an off-line process designed to economically repair defective solder joints or replace defective components. Maintenance can basically be divided into three types of repair welding, heavy work and repair.

  10. Electrical testing

    The electrical test mainly includes online test and functional test. The online test checks whether the connection between each individual component and the test circuit is good. The functional test determines whether the whole circuit can achieve the predetermined function by the working environment of the analog circuit.

  11. Quality Management

    Quality management includes quality control within the production line and product quality assurance prior to delivery to the customer. Mainly to check the process control status of defective products, feedback products and to ensure that the product quality indicators meet customer requirements.

  12. Packaging and sampling inspection

    Finally, the components are packaged and sampled and tested after packaging to ensure the high quality of the products that will be delivered to the customers.
